Beaver Mines


Beaver Mines Campground is found in Castle Provincial Park. It is a hamlet in southern Alberta, located at the foothills of the Canadian Rockies. It has a large lake where you can go boating, fishing, and even swim if you want. It is surrounded by a forest and offers a great view of the mountains. It was once a coal mining town, home to nearly 1500 residents, but due to war efforts needing the steel from the train tracks, it became a ghost town. In recent years it has begun to grow as a popular recreational destination due to the growth of Castle Provincial Park and Calgary. It attracts many people from around the world due to its natural beauty.
